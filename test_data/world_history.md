# World History Notes

## World War II

World War II lasted from 1939 to 1945. It involved most of the world's nations and was the deadliest conflict in human history, with an estimated 70-85 million casualties.

The war began when Nazi Germany, led by Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland on September 1, 1939. Britain and France declared war on Germany in response.

The Allied powers included the United States, the United Kingdom, the Soviet Union, and France. The Axis powers included Germany, Italy, and Japan.

The war in Europe ended on May 8, 1945 (V-E Day) with the defeat of Nazi Germany. The war in the Pacific ended on September 2, 1945 (V-J Day) after the US dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

## The Cold War

The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union from 1947 to 1991. It was called "cold" because there was no direct large-scale fighting between the two powers.

Key events include the Berlin Blockade (1948), the Korean War (1950-1953), the Cuban Missile Crisis (1962), and the Vietnam War (1955-1975).

The Cold War ended with the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991.

## The Renaissance

The Renaissance was a cultural and intellectual movement that began in Italy in the 14th century and spread across Europe through the 17th century.

It marked a renewed interest in classical Greek and Roman culture, art, and learning. Key figures include Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael, and Galileo Galilei.

The invention of the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg around 1440 helped spread Renaissance ideas across Europe.

## The Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century and transformed economies from agrarian and handcraft-based to manufacturing and industry-based.

Key inventions include the steam engine (James Watt), the spinning jenny (James Hargreaves), and the locomotive. These innovations dramatically increased production and changed how people lived and worked.
