# Physics Notes

## Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's First Law: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted on by an external force. This is also called the law of inertia.

Newton's Second Law: Force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma). The greater the mass of an object, the more force is needed to accelerate it.

Newton's Third Law: For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. If you push against a wall, the wall pushes back with the same force.

## Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy of motion. It is calculated as KE = ½mv², where m is mass and v is velocity.

Potential energy is stored energy. Gravitational potential energy is calculated as PE = mgh, where m is mass, g is gravitational acceleration (9.8 m/s²), and h is height.

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another.

## Waves

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy through matter or space. Waves have amplitude, wavelength, and frequency.

Frequency is the number of waves that pass a point per second, measured in hertz (Hz). Wavelength is the distance between two consecutive wave crests.

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 3 × 10⁸ m/s. Light is an electromagnetic wave and does not require a medium to travel.

## Electricity

Ohm's Law states that voltage equals current times resistance: V = IR.

Current is the flow of electric charge, measured in amperes (A). Voltage is the electric potential difference, measured in volts (V). Resistance opposes the flow of current, measured in ohms (Ω).

In a series circuit, current is the same throughout. In a parallel circuit, voltage is the same across each branch.
